## üèõ The Hierarchy: From Swarms to Sovereignty
I have moved away from the "organic swarm" model in favor of a **Command and Control (C2) Architecture**. In this system, AI is organized into five layers of governance:

### 1. The Constitution (The Code of Conduct)
The bedrock of the nation. These are hard-coded Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs). No agent, no matter how "smart," can bypass these rules. It defines the "Legal" limits of what the AI is allowed to do with my data and my money.

### 2. The Executive Branch (The General & The Auditor)
* **The General (Orchestrator):** The high-level strategist. It translates my "Meta-Task" (e.g., "Launch a video campaign") into a visual **Mermaid Battle Plan**.
* **The Auditor (Quality Control):** A separate, high-reasoning entity. It doesn't do the work; it judges it. Nothing reaches me without the Auditor's digital signature.

### 3. Specialized Cities (Dockerized Isolation)
I don't believe in one giant brain. We have specialized "Cities" (isolated Docker domains) for Video, Image, Audio, and Text. 
* **Self-Assembly:** If a City lacks a tool, it doesn't fail. It spawns a **Developer Agent** to build a new microservice, tests it in quarantine, and adds it to the "National Library."

### 4. The Logistics Layer (Event Stream & The Ticket System)
Instead of agents talking *to* each other, they post to a central **Event Stream**. 
* **The Doctor‚Äôs Office Logic:** To prevent race conditions and "knowledge creep," agents must "take a ticket" (distributed locking) to get the latest snapshot, update the global state, and exit the editing step. 
* **The Black Box:** Every decision is logged on an immutable ledger (potentially a high-speed blockchain), creating a permanent "Flight Recorder" of the system‚Äôs logic.

### 5. The Self-Assembling UX (The Interface)
The UI is not a static dashboard; it is a **Generative UI (GenUI)**. It assembles itself in real-time based on the agents' needs. If the General needs a file, a "File Upload" component appears. If the Video Unit finishes, a "Video Player" is rendered. The UI is a mirror of the backend's current mission state.
